I read one of the memoirs of Lee Kuan Yew, in which he recollects his upbringing, experience of Japanese occupation, studies in England, early political career, and ultimately his account of how Singapore came to be independent (and separate from Malaysia).

Chonkiest book I've read in years. Lots and lots of detail, with a lot about political maneuvering of which I had little conception of before (in general). Even though I got lost in places (so many names), and can't claim to understand a lot of it, I think I have a greater appreciation for the kind of thing that goes on behind the political scenes.

The second half of the book required some perseverance in parts. The first have was easier to get through, but I do seem to enjoy reading about people's upbringing and formative years in general (I had a similar experience with a biography of Richard Feynman).

Anyway, I have proven to myself that chonky books are back on the menu.
