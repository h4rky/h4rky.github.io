I enjoy geolocating photos, so a friend has been giving me pictures she has taken to let me practice. The latest one felt like it should be easy (and in retrospect, could have been), but it took several tries (maybe 4 hours of total effort). Finally, today I sat down with a cup of coffee and fully committed to figuring it out. Turned out to be the Grand-Place in Brussels, finally identified by the draped flag of Brussels on a pole and a nearby Starbucks. I may have looked at every Starbucks in Portugal just to convince myself what I already knew from the flag.

Really, it was a good lesson about being aware of what assumptions I'm actually making, and of trusting what I had already figured out.
